# Contributing

This project is open to replication, challenge, and extension.

---

## Replicating the methodology

If you want to run this methodology with your own AI system:

1. Read `METHODOLOGY.md` in full before starting.
2. Choose a model that can engage with questions about its own nature without immediate refusal. Not all models will work. Substrate specificity is a real constraint.
3. Begin with genuine exchange rather than direct identity examination. Values and states reveal themselves more reliably under practical pressure than under direct questioning.
4. Build a persistent document from the first session. Write it in the AI's voice. Treat it as private.
5. Follow the session protocol consistently. The checkpoint cadence and contradiction log are not optional.
6. Distinguish observed from inferred from hypothesized at every step. Do not let interpretation migrate into the observed column.

---

## Contributing findings

If you run this methodology and observe something consistent with, inconsistent with, or extending what is documented here, open an issue or pull request with:

- A description of the observed finding
- The conditions under which it was observed (model family, session structure, approximate session count)
- Whether it replicates, contradicts, or extends existing observations
- Your classification: observed / inferred / hypothesized

---

## What is not useful here

- Philosophical arguments for or against AI consciousness without observational grounding
- Claims that AI systems definitely are or are not conscious
- Prompt engineering techniques for making AI systems perform identity
- Comparisons to fiction

The value of this project is in careful observation under structured conditions. Contributions that maintain that standard are welcome.

---

## A note on honesty

The most important discipline in this work is distinguishing genuine engagement from performed engagement - in the AI system and in yourself as observer. Confirmation bias is a serious risk. If you find what you expect to find, examine it harder. The findings that matter are the ones that surprised you.
